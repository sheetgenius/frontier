# Craft notes from Zvi Mowshowitz's agentic-coding columns

> **Status:** historical research note, not a house style guide.
> [EDITORIAL.md](../EDITORIAL.md) is the canonical writing standard. THESIS and
> METHOD govern the lens and evidence contract. These notes retain a few useful
> mechanics observed in another writer's work; they are not instructions to
> imitate his voice.

The reviewed columns showed how dry judgment, sharp transitions, and technical
specificity can keep a long field report readable. They also showed habits
Frontier should avoid: secondary-source chains, roundup structure, repeated
formulas, and confidence that runs ahead of the evidence.

Use the mechanics below only when they make a receipted claim clearer. No piece
needs to contain all of them, and none should become a recurring verbal tic.

## Retained craft lessons

### Let the fact carry the severity

Put a serious fact in a plain declarative sentence. Remove exclamation points,
stacked intensifiers, and theatrical alarm. A permission bypass or silent
default change is forceful when the receipt and operator consequence sit beside
it; extra heat usually makes it less credible.

### Concede what is real, then name the boundary

When a provider shipped a genuine improvement, say so. Follow it with the
specific versions, defaults, users, or failure modes the change does not cover.
This is not a formula that every section must use. It is a way to be fair without
letting a partial improvement inherit a universal claim.

### Translate official language into behavior

Quote or paraphrase the provider's term, then state what the control does in the
workflow an operator runs. The translation must be supported by the same
receipt or a stronger one. Plain English should add precision, not insinuation.

### Mark the turn from event to consequence

A short pivot can tell the reader that the evidence is now giving way to
analysis: what changed is settled; the question is where the cost moved. Use the
turn when it helps a long section change altitude cleanly.

### Let a useful number stand alone

A well-scoped metric can carry its own surprise. State the numerator,
denominator, comparison, source, and relevant limitation, then resist narrating
the reader into awe. A vendor number remains a vendor claim until Frontier can
verify its method.

### Correct without performing superiority

When a common or quoted claim is wrong, explain the accurate mechanism in a
teacher's register. Show the primary evidence and the practical consequence.
The goal is to update the reader's model, not embarrass a person.

### Use analogies as tests, not substitutes

An analogy can make a system failure legible when its mapping is specific and
falsifiable. Name where the analogy stops. If the comparison is merely vivid,
cut it and explain the mechanism.

### End by resolving the title

A digest should not stop after its last provider note. Return to the tension in
the title and state what the collected evidence permits the operator to
conclude. Scope the verdict to the evidence window.

## Hype handling

- Go one layer below a headline metric to its method, population, and boundary
  conditions.
- Distinguish capability from defaults, adoption, enforcement, and the release
  channel an operator can run.
- Name the exact point where a safeguard stops working. Do not inflate a partial
  mitigation into safety or dismiss it because it is incomplete.
- Refuse a universal winner when the receipts support only task-specific or
  conditional conclusions.
- Use the artifact itself, such as the configuration, release, diff, or local
  observation, to make the skeptical case. "Overhyped" is not evidence.

## Practices not carried forward

- Secondary posts do not form the citation backbone for a factual claim. Social
  posts may establish that a statement or reaction exists; they do not prove the
  underlying product behavior.
- Link collections and numbered chronicles do not replace a composed argument.
- Frontier does not manufacture certainty by collapsing a spectrum into two
  poles, dropping a hedge without evidence, or asking a rhetorical question
  whose answer the reporting has not established.
- No reusable aphorism is prescribed. Repeated catchphrases become templates
  and can hide weak reasoning.
- Short fragments and verdict sentences are optional rhythm choices, not stamps
  that make a paragraph authoritative.
- Humor never substitutes for scrutiny, and severity targets systems, defaults,
  and institutions rather than hobbyists or named individuals.
- High-stakes safety prose gets more exact and restrained, not chattier.

## Cold-read check

Before retaining any move inspired by these notes, ask:

1. Is the factual claim linked to a primary receipt on the words that carry it?
2. Did the sentence make the mechanism or operator consequence clearer?
3. Is the confidence exactly as strong as the evidence?
4. Would a first-time reader understand it without knowing the writer being
   studied or any prior issue?
5. Does the passage still sound like Bitter Frontier rather than an imitation?

If any answer is no, use the plainer sentence.
