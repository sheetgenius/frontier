# OpenHands Source Notes

OpenHands should be watched as the productized software-agent platform source.

The important question is:

```text
What happens when the agent harness becomes a full development platform?
```

High-signal areas:

- SDK, CLI, local GUI, cloud, and enterprise surfaces
- sandboxing, runtime configuration, and security posture
- collaboration, RBAC, permissions, and multi-user operation
- integrations such as GitHub, Slack, Jira, Linear, or browser extensions
- evaluation infrastructure and benchmark methodology
- deployment, self-hosting, and enterprise administration
- where the product simplifies adoption while preserving evidence and control

Do not over-weight:

- generic platform breadth without a concrete operator consequence
- integrations that are convenient but do not change serious work
- benchmark claims without method
- cloud features that cannot be inspected or self-hosted

Editorial stance:

OpenHands is where the frontier asks the platformization question. The reading
worth taking from it is which surfaces make agentic software work adoptable by
teams, and which of those a team should wrap, adapt, or refuse. The danger for
any operator is becoming every surface at once. The lesson is how a serious
agent platform packages runtime, collaboration, sandboxing, evaluation, and
enterprise governance.
