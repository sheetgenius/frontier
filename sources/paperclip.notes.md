# Paperclip Source Notes

Paperclip should be watched as a Factory analogue, not merely as another
multi-agent app.

The important question is:

```text
Can agent labor become operating state a human can govern?
```

High-signal areas:

- goals, roles, org charts, managers, and agent teams
- budgets, cost visibility, and economic constraints
- approvals, accountability, review, and escalation
- progress state that maps to actual work, not dashboard theater
- session and task lineage
- integrations that turn agent activity into operational workflows
- governance primitives that are enforced rather than merely described

Do not over-weight:

- company metaphors without enforceable authority or budget semantics
- generic multi-agent demos
- dashboards that do not change the next action
- claims about autonomous companies without audit trails

Editorial stance:

Paperclip teaches the Factory question. The lesson is not "copy the company
metaphor." The lesson is to ask what makes agent labor economically legible:
what was asked, who or what did it, what budget it consumed, what evidence it
left, what remains blocked, and what a human can approve or redirect.
